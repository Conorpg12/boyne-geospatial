# Boyne Geospatial — Website Overhaul Brief

## Business Context

Boyne Geospatial is pivoting away from GIS mapping and spatial analysis services. This is due to a conflict of interest with one founder's new role at a local council. The business is now focused on **drone-led visual storytelling and content production**, combining drone imagery, ground-level camera work, editorial storytelling, and location intelligence.

### Equipment
- DJI Mavic Mini 4 Pro (drone)
- Canon 90D (ground camera)

### Brand Positioning
The site should be positioned around the concept of **"Storytelling by Place"** — a nod to:
- Drone and media expertise
- GIS and location intelligence background (used as a production differentiator, not a client service)
- Partner's marketing and journalism degree
- Rootedness in the Boyne Valley and Irish landscape

Suggested tagline directions:
- *"Storytelling by Place"*
- *"Where place meets story"*
- *"Stories grounded in place"*

---

## Services to Feature

Remove all references to: GIS Mapping, Spatial Analysis, GeoPackage Viewer, technical planning language, consultancy.

Replace with the following core services. Keep language engaging, warm and accessible — not technical:

1. **New Homes & Property**
   Framed imagery, story reels and images for new home developments and real estate farm shots. Portfolio pieces that sell lifestyle, not just bricks.

2. **Sports Clubs & Events**
   Content and buildup pieces for sports clubs. Match-day coverage, training sessions, pre-season hype reels, club culture storytelling.

3. **Athlete Content**
   Athlete reels and mini documentaries. Following athletes during training with drone and ground camera. Documentary-style interviews. Terrain-intelligent shot planning.

4. **Workplace & Engagement**
   Workplace engagement pieces — team culture, company stories, recruitment content, behind-the-scenes storytelling for brands and organisations.

5. **Progress & Development Documentation**
   Ongoing documentation of construction, development, land, or any project where drone imagery adds unique perspective over time.

6. **Stories & Place**
   Editorial and narrative-driven content about people, places and communities — particularly rooted in the Boyne Valley and Irish landscape.

---

## Site Structure Changes

### Navigation
- Add a **dropdown on the Services nav item** — when hovered, it should show all individual service pages as links
- Each service should have its own **dedicated landing page**

### Homepage
- Reword to reflect the new direction entirely
- Remove all GIS/technical language
- Lead with the storytelling and visual content angle
- Mention drone (Mavic Mini 4 Pro) and camera (Canon 90D) naturally in context
- Reflect the Boyne Valley and Irish landscape as part of the brand identity
- Include a clear call to action

### Services Page
- Should act as an overview/hub with cards linking to each individual service page
- Copy should be engaging and visitor-focused — avoid jargon
- Each card should have a short punchy description and a "Find out more" link

### Individual Service Landing Pages
Create a dedicated page for each of the 6 services listed above. Each page should include:
- Hero section with relevant headline
- Short description of the service
- What's included / what to expect
- Who it's for
- Call to action (contact or enquire)

### About Page
- Update to reflect the new direction
- Mention both founders' complementary backgrounds: GIS/spatial expertise + marketing/journalism degree
- Frame GIS knowledge as a behind-the-scenes production advantage (terrain planning, location scouting)
- Reference the Boyne Valley as home base and inspiration

---

## Tone & Copy Guidelines

- **Warm, confident, and creative** — not corporate or technical
- Speak directly to the visitor: "We'll capture...", "Your story..."
- Avoid: consultancy, spatial analysis, GIS, mapping, data layers, technical outputs
- Use: story, place, perspective, capture, moment, content, reel, documentary
- Short punchy sentences. Let the imagery do the heavy lifting.

---

## Technical Notes

- Site is static HTML/CSS/JS — no frameworks
- Keep all existing CSS variables and design system intact
- Maintain mobile responsiveness throughout
- Dropdown nav should work on both hover (desktop) and tap (mobile)
- Each new service page should follow the same HTML structure as existing pages
- Update all internal links to reflect new pages and nav structure
